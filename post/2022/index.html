Reflections & Resolutions: 2022

Dec 26, 2022 5 min read

2022 has been an eventful year in my professional life but a miserable one in my personal life. I have had a chance to reflect on the past many times throughout the year, and keeping my emotions at bay has been a challenge; nevertheless, I have managed to heal a few wounds, but some are still at large. One of the essential things I learned was through my manager at work, “imagine you have a bucket, and everything you feel is poured into it like drops of water”. There will be a time when the bucket will overflow, so I want to start with the times when it burst and how I bought it under control through the positive news afterwards. I will also discuss some resolutions that I want to exercise this year.

  1. Losing people: No, I have not lost these people from the material world but from life. Four people meant a lot to me, and I lost all of them in a week. I miss them a lot and often think about the good times I had through photos, places I visited and even conversations I have with someone else. I feel apologetic, angry, and regretful because these people put meaning into the city where I am now based. I feel empty and in a mirage because of it.

How did I cope?: It’s been challenging to cope with this and with this massive emptiness. I can only move forward by forgetting about it and keeping myself busy.

What more can I do to repair? : Sadly, I cannot do anything anymore. The best I can do is to wish well for them and, at the same time, keep hope alive (i.e. by keeping my fingers crossed).

  1. Anxiety: I have avoided reading emails or opening my social media. I trace this back to an email I received a year ago. I leave opening them for days, or if I have to open my account, I restrict myself to only sending an email and not reading any emails.

How did I cope?: When urgent, I usually turn to some folks who I trust and ask them to be my support when I read through my emails. I worry about this turning into a habit.

What more can I do to repair? I need to be more resilient to both good and bad news. I recall my mentor at my work mentioning, “you need to remain immune to negative and positive communication”. It does not take time for a negative aspect to become positive and vice-versa. I thought it meant for me to be a robot, so I did not pay heed. However, on reflection, I now realise what he meant, and perhaps it is a life skill I need to develop to get this resilience.

  1. Family: I have realised that I have avoided my family on occasions when my work takes over me. I avoid calls and do not respond to messages. How did I cope?: I did not cope well, and in fact, I realised that the more I avoid my family, the more I am heading towards loneliness. I don’t have much to look forward to besides my family, and I must take time out for them.

  1. Active Listening: For my end-year review this year, one of my colleagues point out that I should actively work towards active listening. He noted a couple of instances when I would ask to repeat or forget, requesting some information I needed for the task. I found some helpful suggestions after going through some articles: listen, don’t interrupt, refer to non-verbal cues, stay focused during and paraphrase and summarise. I plan to use these aspects for self-improvement.

  2. Professionalism: In my first year in a corporate, I have had personal troubles navigating through what is professional and what is not. I would often go into the office, seeing varied behaviours. In my one-to-one with my manager, he mentioned that time is essential in determining the kind of relationship people want for you and the type of relationship I would like to have with them. Often, I would spend time giving my time to people only to be in their good books, but I now realise that I do not only want to be a helpful or a giving person. I want to build credibility for myself as it would do me more good in the long run. So, to act upon this, I have become sterner and less social (not aloof) to the point that it works for me and does not impact my work.

  1. Getting out: This year, I have been out considerably more than I have in the past. I like coding and learning, which means often spending time on screen. My landlady pointed out that it is detrimental to one’s health. So, I picked up cycling, which I enjoyed a lot. I plan to continue this hobby and supplement it further by taking another outdoor sport such as running, badminton, or swimming.

I took this photo while cycling from Marchmont to Peebles, with a stopover at Penicuik for Pizza!

  1. Time towards what I like: I like optimisation and developing software devoted to it. Since being part of two giant corporates, my schedule has led to skipping my interests. This would be a resolution to establish this year as the rest of the points. I hope to produce open-source code and participate in things of interest. Further, this year delivering two sessions I enjoy teaching, so I will make it a point to provide courses on optimisation and programming.
